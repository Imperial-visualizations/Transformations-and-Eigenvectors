Eigenvectors?

When you multiply a matrix by a vector, a new transformed vector is produced. In this visualisation, you can set your own 2x2 matrix and see how it transforms all points (i.e. position vectors) in 2D space.

The eigenvectors of a matrix are vectors whose direction do not change under the matrix transformation. Position vectors along the span (direction) of the eigenvectors always remain on the same line, i.e. they do not change direction, after they are transformed by a matrix.

The eigenvalue is the scaling factor by which each position vector along the eigenvector span is stretched.
